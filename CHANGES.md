# Changes — foral-ownership patch and downstream regeneration

This changelog documents the round of edits introduced to correct the
geographic scope of the interurban road network used by the charging-station
optimizer. Everything in this document is traceable to a line of the datathon
brief (IE–Iberdrola, March 2026) or an external regulation cited inline.

## Motivation

The CNIG "Redes de Transporte" shapefile is the authoritative source for
Spain's interurban road geometry (§4.1 item 1 of the brief), but its
`titulard` column records legal ownership of each segment rather than its
role in the interurban network. Álava, Bizkaia, Gipuzkoa and Navarra
delegate ownership of their AP/A/N corridors to the Diputaciones Forales
and the Gobierno de Navarra under the Concierto Económico (Ley 12/2002) and
the Convenio Económico (Ley 28/1990) respectively. A naïve
`titulard == 'Administración General del Estado'` filter therefore silently
omits every state-highway-equivalent kilometre in those four provinces —
AP-1, AP-8, AP-68 Basque section, A-15 Pamplona–Donostia, and the
N-121 / N-135 / N-240 trunk — and with them every candidate charging site
in a region of 3.7 M inhabitants.

This patch restores those corridors without compromising the rest of the
peninsular filter.

## Patch summary

1. **Foral bounding-box exception** (`02_charging_network/01_load_roads.ipynb`).
   The ownership filter is widened to admit segments whose `titulard` is
   `Diputación Provincial` or `Comunidad Autónoma` **and** whose geometry
   intersects the foral provinces' bounding box `(-3.55, 41.90, -0.70, 43.55)`
   in ETRS89. The existing AP-/A-/N- regex (step 3 of the filter) runs
   unchanged on top, so regional autonomous-community roads with similar
   naming are still excluded outside the foral area.

2. **Re-runnable save cells**. The three save cells now overwrite any
   pre-existing output file rather than skipping. This is a correctness fix:
   once the patch changes what goes into `roads_interurban.geojson`, the
   previous skip-if-exists logic would silently keep stale geometry.

3. **Browser-friendly map layer** (new section 8 of notebook 01). A
   `roads_interurban_mapview.geojson` is generated by dissolving per road,
   applying a Douglas-Peucker simplification at 500 m tolerance, and adding
   a `ten_t` boolean flag sourced from the TEN-T core list in Regulation
   (EU) 1315/2013, Annex I. `bi_visualization/build_map.py` consumes this
   file directly, removing the dependency on a hand-curated `corridors.py`.

4. **Assumptions updated** (`assumptions.md`). Row 7 documents the foral
   exception, its legal basis, and its observed impact (~6% of interurban
   road-km restored, AP-1 / AP-8 / AP-68 / A-15 / N-121 / N-135 / N-240
   added to the placement universe).

## Verification

The patch was executed against the CNIG shapefile on 18 April 2026. Key
numbers from `01_load_roads.ipynb`:

| Metric | Before patch | After patch |
|---|---|---|
| Features after ownership filter | 221,386 | 249,632 |
| Foral-exception segments captured | 0 | 28,246 |
| Foral-exception segments passing AP/A/N regex | 0 | 15,544 |
| Distinct foral AP/A/N roads | 0 | 33 |
| Total interurban km | 48,155 | ~51,000 |

All eight priority foral corridors are present after the patch: AP-1, AP-8,
AP-15, AP-68, A-15, N-121, N-135, N-240.

## Scope — inclusions and explicit exclusions

- **Peninsular Spain** — complete coverage, state-owned + foral exception.
- **Ceuta** — included. CNIG records N-352 / N-354 / N-362 as state-owned
  and they pass the AP/A/N regex unchanged.
- **Canarias, Baleares, Melilla** — excluded. Road administration was
  transferred to the autonomous / insular / city governments
  (Canarias: Ley 9/1991; Baleares: Consells Insulars; Melilla: Ciudad
  Autónoma), which renamed their networks to insular prefixes
  (`TF-`/`GC-`/`LP-`/`LZ-`/`FV-` / `Ma-`/`Me-`/`Ei-`/`PM-` / `ML-`).
  Those codes do not satisfy the "autopistas, autovías, or carreteras
  nacionales as classified in the Ministry of Transport dataset"
  classification that §2 of the brief pins the scope to. The provided DSO
  capacity data (i-DE, Endesa, Viesgo, UFD, E-Redes, Begasa, EASA) is
  likewise peninsular + Ceuta only, so excluding these territories keeps
  the optimization over a region where both demand and supply data are
  well-defined.
