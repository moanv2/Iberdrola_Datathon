1. WHY THIS FILE EXISTS
-----------------------
Objective 2 of the datathon asks for "a geospatial analysis identifying
the specific nodes or regions where grid capacity restricts deployment"
(brief p.4). The JURY-MANDATED deliverable for that objective is File 3
(FRIC_001…FRIC_014), and that file is already in /outputs and is frozen.

File 3 is a 14-row STATION-LEVEL bottleneck log — it tells the jury
where proposed chargers would hit grid-capacity friction. What it does
NOT do is show the wider NETWORK-LEVEL picture: which substations across
Spain should Iberdrola reinforce first, regardless of whether we happen
to have placed a charger there? That wider answer is what this Obj 2
work delivers on top of File 3, via a supplementary layer:

    /outputs/critical_zones.csv            (41 rows, 17 cols)
    /03_grid_viability/figures/critical_zones_map.png

Both artefacts are documented as "innovation beyond the baseline" in the
Analytical Report — the datathon explicitly rewards this kind of
additional analysis (p.12 "additional layers… positively valued";
p.15 T3 "crossing of mobility data with distributor capacity datasets").


2. HOW THE TWO OBJ-2 ARTEFACTS RELATE
-------------------------------------
File_3.csv                    critical_zones.csv
--------------------------    ---------------------------
14 rows                       41 rows
7 mandatory columns           17 analytical columns
Locations = proposed          Locations = every interurban
  charging stations where       substation within 25 km of
  grid_status ∈ {Moderate,      an A-/AP-/N- corridor where
  Congested}                    grid_status ∈ {Moderate, Congested}
JURY SUBMISSION               Internal evidence / BI layer
Do not modify                 May be refined further

A File_3 friction point maps to a File_2 proposed station (same coords,
same grid_status). A critical_zones row maps to a PHYSICAL grid
substation (CNMC R1-001 data) and therefore includes the substation
name, municipio, voltage level, exact MW available, nearest corridor,
demand weight, and a priority score. They are complementary, not
redundant.

4. METHODOLOGY — STEP BY STEP
-----------------------------
 Step A.  Feasibility gate
           grid_nodes.csv → drop rows with capacidad_firme_MW < 0.001
           (same MIN_CAP_MW_BUILD as Obj 1). 5,927 → 626 candidate
           nodes. The ~5,300 excluded nodes are EXACTLY-zero-capacity
           substations; they are physical no-builds until new
           transformers are commissioned and fall outside Obj 2's
           "reinforcement candidate" definition.

 Step B.  Grid-status classification (identical bands to Obj 1)
           capacidad_firme_MW  >  5.0  →  Sufficient
           1.0 ≤ capacidad      ≤  5.0 →  Moderate
           0   < capacidad      <  1.0 →  Congested

 Step C.  Distributor remap (Rule 1 compliance)
           i-DE                            → i-DE
           Naturgy (Madrid/Galicia/CLM)    → i-DE
           Begasa  (Lugo)                  → i-DE
           Endesa                          → Endesa
           Viesgo  (Cantabria/N-Asturias)  → Viesgo
           Eredes  (Asturias)              → Viesgo
           Remap is geographic — the collapsed labels match the true
           licensed service areas of the 3 accepted DSOs.

 Step D.  Spatial join (nodes ⟷ corridors)
           Sample each interurban line every 2 km → 487,465 points.
           For each of the 626 nodes, find nearest sample via cKDTree
           → dist_to_corridor_km, nearest_corridor.
           Drop nodes > 25 km from any corridor (scope rule, brief p.2:
           "interurban transport network"). 626 → 549.

 Step E.  Attach corridor demand
           Merge nearest_corridor → peak_daily_sessions (2027).
           Roman-numeral harmonization (N-II ↔ N-2, etc.) so that
           corridors named differently in MITMA vs. demand table still
           match. 549 → 188 nodes with non-zero demand weight (others
           are on tributary N-roads not in the 19-corridor table).

 Step F.  Deficit and priority
           TARGET_MW = 1.2  (8-charger fast pool = 8 × 150 kW)
           deficit_MW     = max(0, TARGET_MW − capacidad_firme_MW)
           priority_score = deficit_MW × peak_daily_sessions_2027

 Step G.  Filter to critical zones
           Keep rows where:
              grid_status ∈ {Moderate, Congested}  AND
              priority_score > 0
           Sort descending by priority_score. Final universe = 41 rows.

6. HEADLINE FINDINGS
--------------------
 * Total critical zones identified ............ 41
      – Congested (< 1 MW) .................... 32
      – Moderate  (1–5 MW) ....................  9
 * Priority concentration:
      – León           (7 zones, Σ score 136,822) — A-6 / N-630 belt
      – Valencia       (5 zones, Σ score  77,449) — A-3 / N-III
      – Madrid         (7 zones, Σ score  77,013) — A-1 / A-2 / A-3
      – Toledo (prov 7,     2 zones, Σ score 32,706) — A-4
 * Distributor breakdown:
      i-DE   : 22 zones (largest backlog — matches Iberdrola footprint)
      Endesa : 12 zones
      Viesgo :  7 zones
 * Interpretation: Iberdrola should pair every phase-1 charger rollout
   on the A-1/A-2/A-3/A-4/A-6 inland corridors with concurrent grid
   capex in León, Madrid-north, and Valencia. On the coastal AP-7 and
   A-7 routes, grid capacity is largely Sufficient — rollout can
   proceed charger-first.


7. ASSUMPTIONS & JUSTIFICATIONS (FOR ANALYTICAL REPORT)
-------------------------------------------------------
 A1  Grid-status bands (< 1 / 1–5 / > 5 MW) — taken directly from the
     datathon image supplied in the technical pack; same bands as Obj 1.
 A2  MIN_CAP_MW_BUILD = 0.001 — strict zero-MW feasibility gate; any
     positive capacity is treated as "hostable with appropriate sizing".
 A3  TARGET_MW = 1.2 = 8 chargers × 150 kW (Rule 2). This is the "fully
     built-out fast-charging pool" for a trunk corridor site.
 A4  25 km corridor buffer — chosen because a charger placed further
     than ~25 km from an interurban road cannot realistically serve
     long-distance EV drivers (scope rule, brief p.2).
 A5  Priority score = deficit × sessions — gives weight to corridors
     actually used for long-distance travel. Known limitation: a tight
     node on an untracked minor N-road gets score 0; these are flagged
     in the notebook and noted in the report as a secondary priority
     list to be reviewed manually.
 A6  Distributor remap — see §4 Step C; geographically defensible.


8. LIMITATIONS & FUTURE WORK
----------------------------
 * The 2027 demand figures are linear projections from DGT April-2026
   monthly registrations. Sensitivity analysis (±20%) does not change
   the top-15 critical-zone ranking; it does change the absolute
   priority-score values.
 * The 25 km corridor buffer is a hard cut-off; a continuous
   distance-decay weight would be more elegant but doesn't change the
   ranking materially at typical buffer widths.
 * We could not verify actual capacidad_firme changes announced between
   the R1-001 April-2026 snapshot and the datathon deadline; future
   iterations should re-pull the R1-001 files closer to submission.
 * Reinforcement cost is not estimated here. A follow-on cost-per-MW
   layer (using CNMC reference values) would turn the priority list
   into a capex-ranked investment plan.
