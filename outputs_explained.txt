1. THE PIPELINE AT A GLANCE
================================================================

We run four notebooks in order. Each one produces a file the next
one consumes. Notebook 04 now additionally consumes a unified
grid-node database assembled by build_grid_nodes.py from the 7
CNMC R1-001 distributor filings.

  NB 01 — Load roads
      Reads the CNIG national road shapefile, keeps only state-
      owned interurban roads (AP / A / N), and dissolves their
      segments into one feature per road.
      Output: 191 roads, ~48,000 km total.

  NB 02 — Existing chargers
      Parses the public DATEX2 charger feed and spatially filters
      to chargers sitting within 5 km of the roads from NB 01.
      Output: 7,890 existing stations.

  NB 03 — Demand allocation
      Takes the 2027 EV fleet forecast (614,412 vehicles) from the
      forecasting team's SARIMA model, splits it across provinces
      using DGT registration data, then maps it to 19 major road
      corridors with expected 2027 peak-daily charging sessions.

  build_grid_nodes.py (pre-processing, run once)
      Reads the 7 CNMC R1-001 distributor Excel files from
      data/raw/grid_capacity/ and writes a unified 5,927-row
      grid_nodes.csv to data/processed/.

  NB 04 — Station placement
      Produces File_1, File_2, File_3. Combines real road geometry
      from NB 01, existing chargers from NB 02, demand tiers from
      NB 03, and the 5,927 CNMC substation nodes from the grid
      database. Runs a greedy SET COVER optimization with an
      empirical grid-capacity feasibility gate.

IBE_001 closed the largest coverage gap; the last IBE entry closed

5. HOW WE CHOSE THE CATEGORICAL / DERIVED VALUES
================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------
5.1  grid_status   (REWRITTEN for v2 — empirical capacity bands)
--------------------------------------------------------

The datathon fixes the three allowed labels but lets us choose the
classification rule, as long as the thresholds are documented
(Rule 1).

v2 replaces the old distance-to-city proxy with empirical capacity
bands applied to the CNMC R1-001 'capacidad firme disponible (MW)'
at each station's NEAREST grid node:

    Sufficient  if  capacidad_firme_MW > 5
    Moderate    if  1 <= capacidad_firme_MW <= 5
    Congested   if  capacidad_firme_MW < 1

Source for thresholds: the datathon parameter image from the IE-
Iberdrola brief. Same bands are used by i-DE's public 'Mapa de
Capacidad de Consumo' (2025).

No assumptions about rural vs urban. If a rural station sits near
a 132 kV feeder with 15 MW free it is correctly labelled Sufficient;
if a peri-urban station sits near a saturated 20 kV node with
0.7 MW free it is correctly labelled Congested. The user's explicit
guidance: *don't just assume that because it's rural it's congested*.

--------------------------------------------------------
5.2  distributor_network   (i-DE / Endesa / Viesgo)
--------------------------------------------------------

Only these three labels are accepted (Rule 1).

We keep the geographic zone rule from v1, with a small remap
addition: substation filings from DSOs outside the 3-enum set
(Begasa/Eredes/Naturgy) still inform capacity and voltage but the
distributor LABEL is resolved to one of the 3 legal enums by
geography:

    Viesgo:   lat > 43.0  AND  -6.5 < lon < -3.0
              (Cantabria + Asturias)

    Endesa:   Catalunya        lon > 0.5   AND lat > 40.5
              Aragón           -1.5 < lon <= 0.5 AND lat > 40.5
              Andalucía east   lat < 38   AND lon > -2.5

    i-DE:     everything else (default — Iberdrola's historical
              centre-west peninsula service area).

Concession polygon data is not open-licensed, so zone-based
assignment is the standard public-data proxy.

--------------------------------------------------------
5.3  n_chargers_proposed   (REWRITTEN v3 — pure grid_status tier)
--------------------------------------------------------

In v3, n_chargers is determined SOLELY by grid_status. The v4
voltage cap and physical-headroom cap have been removed because
no team member could verify the 2/4/8 voltage cap in any of the
documents supplied by the datathon, and the resulting sizing was
too conservative for an Iberdrola 2027 deployment.

The new tier comes directly from the team-supplied image
("Low/Medium/High" capacity bins → charger counts):

  grid_status  | capacidad_firme_MW | n_chargers | demand_kw
  ------------ | ------------------ | ---------- | ---------
  Congested    | < 1 MW             |    4       |    600
  Moderate     | 1 - 5 MW           |    8       |  1,200
  Sufficient   | 5 - 10 MW          |   12       |  1,800
  Sufficient   | > 10 MW            |   16       |  2,400

The image showed "12 o 16" for the High band; we split at
10 MW so the full design range is used. Both 12-charger and
16-charger sites appear in File_2 — the 16-charger ones cluster
on the genuinely high-capacity Sufficient nodes (typically
40+ kV, 15-40 MW available).

Because KW_PER_CHARGER = 150 is fixed by Rule 2, site power is
deterministic and Rule-2-compliant by construction:
    4  x 150  =    600 kW
    8  x 150  =  1,200 kW
    12 x 150  =  1,800 kW
    16 x 150  =  2,400 kW

Corridor-demand tercile (High/Medium/Low) is still computed
in the notebook's `tier_map` for analytics and the strategic
narrative, but it does NOT cap n_chargers anymore.

Congested sites (0 < cap < 1 MW) are still proposed at 4
chargers and flagged in File_3. The Obj 2 critical_zones.csv
analysis identifies these as priority reinforcement targets
(see /obj2_explained.txt §4). The implicit assumption — that
Iberdrola's charger capex is paired with grid capex on the
2027 horizon — must be stated in the Analytical Report.

--------------------------------------------------------
5.4  location selection
--------------------------------------------------------

Set Cover formulation:
    Universe U  = every 1 km point along the interurban road
                  network (~256k points).
    Candidates  = potential sites every 5 km along those roads
                  (~237k candidates).

Pre-cover: every universe point within COVERAGE_RADIUS_KM of an
existing fast-charging hub (n_connectors >= 4) is marked already
covered before optimization.

New in v2 — feasibility gate: every candidate is attached to its
nearest substation node (within 10 km). Candidates where the best
node has capacity <= 0 MW are DROPPED. This enforces the user's
rule: "if == 0 MW, no available power, no station".

Greedy algorithm: repeatedly pick the single candidate that covers
the most still-uncovered universe points. Proven ln(|U|)+1
approximation (Feige 1998).

--------------------------------------------------------
5.5  Feasibility gate (NEW)
--------------------------------------------------------

After candidate generation and before optimization:

  For every candidate, find all grid nodes within NODE_SEARCH_RADIUS_KM
  (10 km). Take the node with maximum available MW (ties broken by
  highest voltage).

  DROP if either:
    (a) no grid node within 10 km (deep infrastructure vacuum), OR
    (b) the best node has capacity < MIN_CAP_MW_BUILD (0.001 MW,
        i.e. effectively zero — enforces user's strict zero rule).

Rows in the audit file (File_3_audit.csv) show:
    nearest_node_cap_MW        (the evidence for grid_status and for
                                the Congested/Moderate/Sufficient tier
                                that drives sizing — see §5.3 v3)
    nearest_node_voltage_kV    (contextual only in v3; retained for
                                the grid-viability team's reinforcement
                                studies but no longer a sizing cap)
    nearest_node_dist_km       (how far the nearest feasible node is)

These are kept OUT of the jury File_3.csv to respect the strict
Section 5 schema, but the grid-viability team uses them for
Obj 2 reinforcement studies.

6. DATATHON RULES WE HAD TO RESPECT
================================================================

  Rule 1   grid_status must be one of
           {Sufficient, Moderate, Congested}.
           distributor_network must be one of
           {i-DE, Endesa, Viesgo}.
           Any other label -> disqualification.

  Rule 2   estimated_demand_kw = n_chargers_proposed x 150.
           150 kW per charger is fixed. Do not change.

  Rule 3   File 3 contains ONLY Moderate or Congested rows.
           Sufficient stations are excluded.

Additional self-imposed constraints:
  - Stations must sit on interurban roads (AP / A / N). Urban
    streets excluded.
  - All forecasts target end of 2027.
  - NEW: stations must have a feasible grid node (cap > 0) within
    10 km. Else the candidate is dropped.

7. PARAMETER TABLE — WHAT WE SET AND WHY
================================================================

  MAX_SPACING_KM  =  50
      20% below the EU AFIR-2023 regulatory floor (60 km on
      TEN-T core network for >=150 kW pools).

  COVERAGE_RADIUS_KM  =  25
      Half of MAX_SPACING_KM. A road point is covered if some
      station sits within this radius.

  CANDIDATE_SPACING_KM  =  5
      Generation granularity along each road.

  MIN_DIST_EXISTING_KM  =  15
      Candidates within 15 km of an existing fast hub are
      dropped before optimization (prevents redundant spend).

  FAST_HUB_MIN_CONNECTORS  =  4
      Proxy for a DC fast-charging hub. ~2,400 existing sites
      qualify across Spain — matches Iberdrola/Repsol public
      counts.

  NODE_SEARCH_RADIUS_KM  =  10    (NEW in v2)
      Max distance from candidate to nearest usable grid node.
      Beyond 10 km, connection cost/ permitting are economically
      unrealistic for an MV/HV station.

  MIN_CAP_MW_BUILD  =  0.001      (NEW in v2)
      Strict-zero gate. Only candidates where the best node
      within 10 km reports exactly 0 MW are dropped
      (user's rule: "== 0 MW means no station"). Congested
      sites with any positive capacity (even 0.05 MW) are still
      proposed and flagged for Obj 2 reinforcement.

  MW_SUFFICIENT  =  5.0   (NEW in v2)   // > 5 MW -> Sufficient
  MW_MODERATE_MIN  =  1.0 (NEW in v2)   // 1-5 MW -> Moderate
                                        // < 1 MW -> Congested

  KW_PER_CHARGER  =  150
      Fixed by Rule 2. Not a design choice.

  EV_RANGE_KM  =  300
      Fleet-weighted 2024-2026 WLTP average ~400 km, times 0.75
      real-world derating. Sanity-check only.

  TRIPS_PER_MONTH  =  3        (NB 03 only)
      DGT baseline * 1.2x gas-price premium. See NB 03 for the
      full empirical chain.

8. GRID-NODE DATABASE (NEW)

Source files (all in data/raw/grid_capacity/):
  i-DE       R1-001  3,017 nodes
  Endesa 1   R1-026      5 nodes
  Endesa 2   R1-299  1,839 nodes
  Viesgo     R1-005    177 nodes
  Begasa     R1-003     23 nodes
  Eredes     R1-008    187 nodes
  Naturgy    R1-002    679 nodes
  ----------------------------------
  Total (after NaN filtering):   5,927 nodes

Key columns we use:
  lat / lon                  (reprojected from UTM)
  capacidad_firme_MW         (float — feasibility gate AND, in v3,
                              the sole driver of n_chargers via the
                              image-based grid_status tier in §5.3)
  voltage_kv                 (int — contextual/audit only in v3; no
                              longer caps charger count)
  distributor                (7 DSOs, remapped to 3 legal enums)
  subestacion / municipio    (audit trail)

Capacity distribution across all 5,927 nodes:
  Congested  (<1 MW):   5,426  (92%)
  Moderate   (1-5 MW):    252  (4%)
  Sufficient (>5 MW):     249  (4%)

Implication: most of Spain's current transmission infrastructure
has very limited firm capacity for new demand. This is why the
empirical placement gives a smaller station count than an
assumption-based approach would — the gate is honest about where
the grid CAN host new load today.

Coordinate handling: i-DE uses European decimal comma
(e.g. "499100,028..."); build_grid_nodes.py coerces this with a
locale-safe to_num_eu() helper. All 7 filings reprojected to WGS84
via pyproj (UTM 30N for mainland, 29N for Galicia).

Rebuilding: run
    python 02_charging_network/build_grid_nodes.py
from the Iberdrola_Datathon root. Takes about 10 seconds.


================================================================
9. KNOWN LIMITATIONS
================================================================

(a) Total station count is bounded by the empirical feasibility
    gate. Where the strict zero-gate drops candidates, the greedy
    Set Cover cannot cover those road points — they surface as
    "disconnected outliers" in the optimization log. This is a
    feature, not a bug: the algorithm is honest about what can
    physically be built without reinforcement.

    The Analytical Report should flag the % of gap universe that
    could not be closed at today's grid capacity as evidence for
    Obj 2's reinforcement case.

(b) distributor_network is assigned by lat/lon rectangles, not
    real concession polygons. Boundary cases on zone edges may
    flip. Fine at corridor scale, imperfect at individual-
    customer scale.

(c) FAST_HUB_MIN_CONNECTORS = 4 remains a public-data proxy.
    Some real DC fast hubs have 2 connectors; some 4-connector
    sites are slow AC. Error cancels on average but is imperfect.

(d) Site sizing uses the 19-corridor tercile split. Roads outside
    that set default to Medium (4 chargers). Conservative — not a
    claim of demand parity with the main A-roads.

(e) The grid-node database is a snapshot of early-2026 CNMC
    filings. Real grid capacity changes monthly as interconnection
    requests close out. A production deployment would re-run
    build_grid_nodes.py on each refresh.

(f) Three DSOs (Begasa/Eredes/Naturgy) contribute grid intelligence
    but have no direct mapping to the 3 legal datathon enums. We
    remap by geography — defensible at corridor scale, but a
    real-world plan would use the DSO's own concession polygon.
